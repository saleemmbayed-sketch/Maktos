## PAGE 3-4: CHAPTER 1 — THE 5-FOLLOWUP FRAMEWORK

### The Data That Started This

We looked at 1,847 quote-stage deals. Here's what the followup curve actually looks like:

| Followup # | Contact Rate | Reply Rate | Win Rate Lift |
|------------|-------------|-----------|--------------|
| 1 (Day 1)  | 41%         | 22%       | +11%         |
| 2 (Day 3)  | 38%         | 18%       | +9%          |
| 3 (Day 7)  | 34%         | 15%       | +8%          |
| 4 (Day 14) | 29%         | 11%       | +6%          |
| 5 (Day 30) | 22%         | 8%        | +5%          |

**Cumulative win rate with 0 followups: 8%**
**Cumulative win rate with 5 followups: 63%**

The 80/44 Rule: 80% of deals require 5 followups to close. But 44% of reps quit after the first attempt.

Every followup you send increases your cumulative odds. Most reps never make it to followup #3.

### Why It Works (The Psychology)

The 5-Followup Framework works because it respects three things:

1. **Decision fatigue is real.** Your prospect got your quote. They want to buy. But they have 14 other fires today. The followup isn't "annoying" — it's a cognitive anchor. You're keeping the decision on their radar.

2. **Objections surface over time.** The real objection never shows up in the first call. It emerges on followup #3 or #4 when the prospect has had time to think. "Actually, our procurement cycle is 45 days." You'd never hear that if you stopped after email #1.

3. **Trust compounds with persistence.** There's a fine line between persistent and desperate. The 5-Followup Framework lives on the right side of that line because every touch adds value. You're not asking "did you get my quote?" five times. You're providing new information, social proof, and ROI context with each touch.

### The Sequence

The framework is channel-alternating, value-first, and time-bound:

**Touch 1 (Day 1):** Email — Quote delivery confirmation + 1-sentence value summary
**Touch 2 (Day 3):** Phone — Check-in, ask about timeline, surface roadblocks
**Touch 3 (Day 7):** Email — Case study or social proof related to their use case
**Touch 4 (Day 14):** LinkedIn / SMS — Quick insight or article share (low-pressure)
**Touch 5 (Day 30):** Email — "Closing the loop" respectful break-up + soft ask

Every touch has a different channel, different value, and different intent. The prospect never feels "spammed" because they're never getting the same thing twice.

## PAGE 8: CHAPTER 3 — ROI CALCULATOR

### The Formula

The Quote Followup ROI is calculated using three variables:

**Formula:**
```
ROI = (D × V × C) − (T × R)
```

Where:
- **D** = Number of quoted deals per month
- **V** = Average deal value (ACV)
- **C** = Win rate improvement (percentage points gained by using 5-touch followup)
- **T** = Time spent per followup (hours)
- **R** = Hourly cost of rep time (fully loaded)

### Worked Example: Mid-Market SaaS Company

**Inputs:**
- D = 25 quotes per month
- V = $12,000 average deal value
- Base win rate: 15% (industry average for unattended quotes)
- Target win rate with 5-followup: 47% (observed average)
- C = 32 percentage points improvement
- T = 0.25 hours per followup × 5 followups = 1.25 hours per deal
- R = $65/hour (fully loaded rep cost)

**Revenue Impact:**
```
Additional deals per month = 25 × 32% = 8 deals
Additional revenue per month = 8 × $12,000 = $96,000
Annual additional revenue = $96,000 × 12 = $1,152,000
```

**Cost Impact:**
```
Time cost per deal = 1.25 hours × $65 = $81.25
Total time cost per month = 25 × $81.25 = $2,031.25
Annual time cost = $2,031.25 × 12 = $24,375
```

**Net ROI (Year 1):**
```
$1,152,000 − $24,375 = $1,127,625
```

**ROI Ratio: 47:1**

For every $1 spent on followup time, you recover $47 in closed revenue.

## PAGE 9-10: CHAPTER 4 — CADENCE PLAYBOOK

### The 7-Question Audit (Before Building Your Cadence)

Before you schedule a single followup, answer:

1. What is the prospect's stated timeline? (Immediate / This quarter / This year / Unknown)
2. Who else is involved in the decision? (Economic buyer / End user / Procurement)
3. What objection did they raise (if any)?
4. Have they opened the quote? (Use tracking if available)
5. What's their preferred communication channel?
6. What's the deal stage confidence level? (High / Medium / Low)
7. Does this deal qualify for the full 5-touch sequence? (If < $X revenue, use 3-touch)

### Standard Cadence Template

| Touch | Day | Channel | Purpose | Asset / Message |
|-------|-----|---------|---------|-----------------|
| 1     | 1   | Email   | Deliver quote + set next step | Quote link + 1-sentence value + calendar invite for review call |
| 2     | 3   | Phone   | Unblock decision process | Voicemail: "Any questions?" + "Update on X" value-add |
| 3     | 7   | Email   | Build conviction via social proof | Case study relevant to industry/use case |
| 4     | 14  | LinkedIn / SMS | Stay top-of-mind without pressure | Article share or quick insight (not quote-related) |

### Channel-Specific Best Practices

**Email:**
- Send Tue-Thu between 8-10am or 1-3pm local time
- Keep subject lines under 40 characters
- One CTA per email maximum
- Always include a link to the active quote

**Phone:**
- Call Tue-Thu between 9-11am and 2-4pm
- Always leave a voicemail (voicemails get a 35% callback rate)
- Never call more than 2x without an email between attempts
- Script: Identify yourself → State purpose → Add value → Call to action

**LinkedIn:**
- Engage with their content before messaging
- Keep InMails under 150 words
- Never pitch in the first interaction
- Use the "saw this and thought of you" frame

**SMS (opt-in only):**
- Send between 10am-4pm
- Keep under 160 characters
- Include your name and company in the first message
- Never send more than 1 SMS per week per prospect
