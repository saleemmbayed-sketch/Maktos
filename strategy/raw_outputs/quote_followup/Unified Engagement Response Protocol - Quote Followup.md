## 3. Escalation Protocol

### 3A — Escalation to Legal Compliance Checker

**Escalate immediately when**:

| Trigger | Example | Channel(s) | Response Time |
|---------|---------|-----------|---------------|
| GDPR/subject rights request | "Delete my data", "Who gave you my email?" | Any | Within 2 hours — Legal Compliance Checker handles |
| CAN-SPAM/compliance complaint | "I never consented to this email" | Email | Within 2 hours — forward to Legal Compliance Checker |
| DMCA / copyright claim | Someone claims your post uses their image | LinkedIn, Twitter | Pause content, escalate within 1 hour |
| Competitor legal threat | "Cease and desist — your claims about us are false" | Any | Escalate immediately. Do not reply. |
| Regulatory inquiry | Prospect is a government/regulatory body | Any | Escalate immediately. Do not reply. |
| Data breach suspicion | Prospect reports receiving email they shouldn't have | Email | Escalate within 1 hour. Pause all sends. |
| Canadian prospect complaint (CASL) | Canadian prospect says they didn't consent | Email | Escalate within 2 hours |

**Escalation mechanics**:
1. Screenshot the interaction. Capture timestamps, full thread, channel context.
2. Tag Legal Compliance Checker in the campaign tracking sheet with: `[LEGAL-ESCALATION]` prefix.
3. Send a message in the team channel: `LEGAL ESCALATION: [brief description] — [link to evidence]. Responded by [time].`
4. Do not reply further to the prospect until Legal Compliance Checker reviews.
5. Legal Compliance Checker acknowledges within 2 hours and provides response guidance.

### 3B — Escalation to Brand Guardian

**Escalate when**:

| Trigger | Example | Action |
|---------|---------|--------|
| Voice inconsistency | "This reply doesn't sound like our brand" or "Too aggressive for our tone" | Tag Brand Guardian for review within 2 hours. Pause additional replies to that thread. |
| Visual/brand asset misuse | Someone on the team uses wrong logo, wrong colors, off-brand imagery | Tag Brand Guardian. Correct immediately. |
| Brand impersonation | Someone pretending to be Quote Followup on any channel | Escalate immediately. Legal Compliance Checker also notified. |
| Off-brand messaging approved by mistake | A post or reply goes live that violates the Brand Framework | Pull it down if possible. Tag Brand Guardian for corrective guidance. |
| Voice drift pattern | Multiple recent replies feel "too salesy" or "too generic" | Engagement Manager flags to Brand Guardian for mid-campaign voice recalibration. |

**Brand Guardian reference**: The Brand Framework document (Quote Followup Brand Framework.md) defines voice as: Authoritative not Arrogant, Operationally Precise, Urgent without Panic. Tone should vary by context (see Section 3 of Brand Framework). All responses should avoid: "just checking in," "spam," "AI magic," "disruptive," "cold outreach."

**Brand Guardian checklist for every response**:
- [ ] Does this sound like a peer, not a vendor?
- [ ] Is there a data point or specific claim, not a platitude?
- [ ] Would a VP of Sales reading this think "this person understands my world"?
- [ ] Is the tone appropriate for the channel? (LinkedIn = professional/consultative, Twitter = conversational/punchy, Reddit = peer-to-peer/humble)

### 3C — Crisis Response: Negative Viral Attention Protocol

**Definition of crisis-level event**:
- A post/thread/reply receiving >100 negative engagements (downvotes, negative replies, angry reactions) within 4 hours
- A public accusation of wrongdoing (spam, scam, misleading claims) gaining traction
- Multiple high-profile accounts amplifying negative sentiment
- Journalist or industry analyst engagement with negative framing

**Immediate response (within 30 minutes of detection)**:

| Step | Action | Owner |
|------|--------|-------|
| 1 | Detect and confirm. Monitor velocity — is it accelerating? | Engagement Manager |
| 2 | **Do not delete the post** (unless it violates platform ToS or contains factually incorrect info). Deleting makes it worse. | All agents |
| 3 | Tag Brand Guardian + Legal Compliance Checker in crisis channel. | Engagement Manager |
| 4 | Draft a holding response if warranted: "We're seeing the conversation around [topic]. We'll address concerns with data and context shortly." Post as a reply to the thread or as a new post addressing it directly. | Brand Guardian + Engagement Manager |
| 5 | Pause all other campaign engagement on that channel until crisis is contained. | Respective channel agent |
| 6 | Investigate root cause: Did we post something misleading? Was a competitor response taken out of context? Did we violate community rules? | Engagement Manager |
| 7 | Within 2 hours: Post a substantive response (ownership if wrong, data/context if misunderstood, correction if error). | Brand Guardian + Legal Compliance Checker approve |
| 8 | Monitor for 24 hours. If de-escalating, resume normal engagement gradually. If still escalating, consider: (a) private outreach to key critics, (b) broader public statement, (c) temporary channel pause. | Engagement Manager |

**Response templates for crisis scenarios**:

**If we made a factual error**:
> We got this wrong. [Specific correction]. We've updated the [post/email/page] to reflect the correct information. Appreciate the community holding us accountable.

**If our post was misinterpreted**:
> We see how this came across differently than intended. For clarity: [restate the actual point, removing ambiguous language]. We've taken notes and will be more precise in future posts.

**If we're accused of spam/astroturfing**:
> Fair criticism. We should have been more transparent about our affiliation from the start. To be clear: [Name] works with Quote Followup, a tool that automates this process. The data shared is from real analysis. We'll include clearer disclosure going forward.

**Post-crisis debrief (within 48 hours of resolution)**:
- Document timeline, actions taken, outcomes
- Update this protocol with learnings
- Legal Compliance Checker files a report for campaign records

## 5. Cross-Platform Conversation Tracking

### 5A — Tracking Multi-Channel Engagement

**When a prospect engages on more than one channel, flag them as "Hot" in the tracking sheet.**

| Pattern | Signal Strength | Action |
|---------|---------------|--------|
| LinkedIn comment → Email open | Medium | Add to DM outreach list. Reference their LinkedIn comment in next email. |
| Email reply → LinkedIn profile view | Medium | Send LinkedIn connection request with personalized note referencing their email reply. |
| Reddit comment → Playbook download | Medium-High | Flag for nurture sequence acceleration. Send email N2 (case study) instead of N1. |
| Twitter mention → Website visit | Medium | Reply on Twitter with playbook link + "Let me know if you want to go deeper." |
| Playbook download → LinkedIn DM open | High | Calendar the demo booking email. Prospect is actively evaluating. |
| Email open (3+) → LinkedIn engagement with post | High | Direct DM on LinkedIn: "Noticed you've been checking out the content — want to just jump on a 10-min call?" |
| Same prospect on 3+ channels in 48 hours | **Critical** | Escalate to Engagement Manager. Prioritize for same-day demo booking outreach. |

- Columns: Prospect Name, Company, Channel 1 (type + date), Channel 2 (type + date), Channel 3, Signal Score (1-5), Next Action, Owner
- LinkedIn Insight Tag + GA4 for web visits tied to email opens
- Manual cross-referencing at daily standup ("Anyone seen [Company Name] on another channel?")

### 5B — Lead Qualification Signals

**What to look for across channels**:

| Signal | Channel(s) | Meaning | Action |
|--------|-----------|---------|--------|
| "We lose deals on followup too" | Any | Self-identified pain match | Move to nurture sequence, offer demo |
| "How much does it cost?" | Any | Active consideration | Send pricing + demo booking |
| "We use [CRM] — does it integrate?" | Any | Implementation mindset | Confirm integration + offer technical walkthrough |
| "We already handle this manually but it's inconsistent" | LinkedIn, Reddit | Ideal prospect — they know they have a gap | DM/email with case study + demo CTA |

## 6. Response Quality Guidelines

### 6A — Brand Voice Checklist for Every Response

Before posting any response, verify:

**Voice Fit**
- [ ] Does this sound like a peer (sales/ops leader), not a vendor?
- [ ] Would the recipient feel like I understand their world?
- [ ] Is there a data point or specific example grounding the claim?
- [ ] Is the tone appropriate for the channel? (LinkedIn: consultative | Twitter: punchy | Reddit: humble peer | Email: helpful)

**Vocabulary Check**
- [ ] No "just checking in" or "any updates?" — weakest followup language
- [ ] No "AI magic," "revolutionary," "disruptive" — empty hype
- [ ] No "spam" or "drip campaign" — implies mindless volume
- [ ] No "cold outreach" — this is quote followup, warmer than cold
- [ ] Using preferred terms: "structured cadence," "revenue leakage," "deal velocity," "execution gap," "quote-to-close rate"

**Tone Check**
- [ ] If positive response: Warm + specific ("Glad this resonated because of [specific insight]")
- [ ] If skeptical response: Respectful + data-backed ("Fair point. Here's what we found...")
- [ ] If question: Direct answer first, then open-ended followup
- [ ] If competitor comparison: Acknowledge competitor's strengths first, then differentiate

**Structural Check**
- [ ] Addressed the person by name where appropriate (LinkedIn, email, Reddit)
- [ ] Response matches the intent of the incoming message (question, compliment, complaint)
- [ ] No generic one-liners — each response shows you read and understood their specific comment
- [ ] CTA is appropriate to the scenario (link, question, offer to help)
- [ ] Spam/negative: Did I disengage appropriately? No arguing.

### 6B — Do Not Say / Always Say Framework

| Do Not Say | Why | Always Say Instead |
|-----------|-----|-------------------|
| "Just checking in" | Weakest followup — adds zero value, asks prospect to do work | Reference something specific + add value: "Thinking about your question on pricing — here's what we've seen work for teams your size." |
| "I don't want to be a bother" | Undermines your position before you start | "If the timing isn't right, no problem — the playbook link is always live." |
| "We're revolutionizing sales" | Empty hype — no B2B buyer believes this | "We built a system that recovers 15-30% of pipeline that typically leaks after the quote is sent." |
| "Sorry for the spam" | You just called your own message spam — why should they read it? | Don't apologize for reaching out. Lead with value. |
| "Any updates on your end?" | Lazy, generic, puts burden on buyer | "Where is this on your priority list for Q3?" or "What would need to be true for this to move forward?" |
| "I know you're busy" | Everyone is busy. It's filler. | Remove entirely. Get to the point. |
| "Per my last email" | Passive-aggressive tone | "Wanted to circle back on this — happy to hop on a quick call if that's easier." |
| "We're different from [competitor]" | Implies you're thinking about competitors, not the buyer's problem | "For the specific job of post-quote followup automation, we focus on one thing: making sure every sent quote gets a structured 5-touch cadence automatically." |
| "Hope you're doing well" | Generic opener that wastes the first impression | Start with context: "Saw your post about followup challenges — our data shows exactly why that pain is real." |
| "We wanted to reach out" | Passive voice. Who is "we"? | Direct: "I put together a playbook on quote followup..." |

### 6C — Personalization Minimum Standard

Every response must clear this personalization bar:

**Minimum** (required for ALL responses):
- Use the person's name (or handle for Twitter/Reddit where name isn't available)
- Reference something specific they said or asked — prove you read their message, not just your script
- Match channel tone: LinkedIn = full sentences, Twitter = conversational brevity, Reddit = peer-to-peer

**Medium** (required for DMs, email replies, and skeptical responses):
- Reference their context: company, role, industry, or a specific challenge they mentioned
- Use a data point or example relevant to their situation
- Offer a specific next action based on their unique response

**Maximum** (required for demo-ready prospects and Tier 1 accounts):
- Reference their specific LinkedIn activity, post, or company news
- Connect your data/insight directly to their stated pain
- Personalize the playbook offer: "The ROI calculator on page 8 is set up for teams like yours — I'd plug in [their deal volume] and see what comes out."

**Quality gate**: Before sending any DM, email reply, or significant public response, ask: "If this person received 10 of these today, would mine stand out?" If the answer is no, personalize more.

### 6D — Response Quality Audit

**Weekly audit by Engagement Manager** (every Friday at 4:00 PM):

1. Random sample of 10 responses per channel agent for the week
2. Score each against the checklist in 6A (pass/fail per item)
3. Track: % of responses passing all voice checks
4. Target: >90% passing rate
5. If <80%: Retraining session scheduled with the agent, focused on specific gaps

### Response Decision Tree

```
Incoming engagement
├── Is it spam/harassment?
│   ├── Yes → Do not engage. Log. Escalate if repeated/harassing.
│   └── No → Continue
├── Is it positive?
│   ├── Yes → Thank + deepen relationship (ask a question, share a data point)
│   └── No → Continue
├── Is it a question?
│   ├── Yes → Answer directly first → Offer demo/resources → Ask a followup
│   └── No → Continue
├── Is it skeptical/challenging?
│   ├── Yes → Acknowledge validity → Share data → No hard sell
│   └── No → Continue
├── Is it a competitor comparison?
│   ├── Yes → Acknowledge competitor strength → Differentiate on focus → Offer comparison call
│   └── No → Continue
└── Is it negative/viral?
    ├── Yes → Crisis Protocol (Section 3C)
    └── No → Standard response per brand voice
```

### Escalation Decision Tree

```
Engagement received
├── Legal concern? (GDPR, CAN-SPAM, DMCA, threat)
│   ├── Yes → Escalate to Legal Compliance Checker (Section 3A). Pause reply.
│   └── No → Continue
├── Brand voice concern? (off-tone, inconsistent, asset misuse)
│   ├── Yes → Escalate to Brand Guardian (Section 3B). Pause if needed.
│   └── No → Continue
├── Viral negative? (>100 negative engagements, journalist, accusation)
│   ├── Yes → Crisis Protocol (Section 3C). Notify Engagement Manager.
│   └── No → Continue
└── Standard engagement → Respond per templates (Section 2)
```
