# Quote Followup 7-Day Lead Sprint Operator Pack

## Mission
Generate `10 qualified leads by next week` using the fastest available channels:
- LinkedIn DMs
- Cold email
- LinkedIn lead form ads

## Offer
`Free 15-Minute Quote Followup Gap Audit`

What the audit covers:
- Where post-quote deals stall
- Whether follow-up is rep-dependent
- Where automation could recover pipeline inside the current workflow or CRM

## Qualified Lead Definition
A lead counts only if all 4 are true:
1. ICP match: VP Sales, Head of Sales, RevOps Director, Sales Ops leader, Inside Sales Manager, or equivalent
2. Company fit: B2B team with quote-driven pipeline and meaningful sales complexity
3. Pain present: they acknowledge a follow-up, stalled quote, visibility, or rep-consistency problem
4. Action taken: they book a meeting, request the audit, or ask for workflow-specific detail

### Minimum Day 1 production targets
- 160 prospects sourced
- 40 LinkedIn connection requests

### Day 1 success criteria
- All 3 channels live
- At least 1 positive signal by end of day
- Tomorrow's follow-ups queued
- No inbound reply left unanswered

## Failure Protocol if by 2 PM There Are Zero Positive Signals
At 2 PM, if there are zero positive replies, zero warm objections, and weak acceptance/open rates:
1. Narrow ICP to RevOps Director, Sales Ops leader, and Inside Sales Manager
2. Replace generic opener with direct pain: `Most teams don't lose quoted deals on pricing. They lose them in the follow-up gap after the quote goes out.`
3. Simplify CTA to `Open to a quick 15-minute audit?`
4. Send 15 highly personalized messages to best-fit accounts
5. Test sharper subjects: `Quote follow-up gap`, `After the quote goes out...`, `Reps stop after follow-up #1`
6. Relaunch revised LinkedIn and email batch by 3 PM

## Segment Variants

### VP Sales angle
Your team may not have a top-of-funnel problem - you may have a post-quote execution problem.

When follow-up after the quote is inconsistent, forecasted deals slip, cycle times stretch, and reps leave revenue on the table.

### RevOps Director angle
This is usually a process visibility issue more than a rep effort issue.

After the quote goes out, most teams lack a reliable system for next-step automation, follow-up compliance, and clean reporting on quote-to-close behavior.

### Inside Sales Manager angle
This usually shows up as a rep consistency problem.

Some reps follow up hard after sending a quote. Others send one message and move on. We help managers put a structured follow-up system in place so deals don't depend on individual rep habits.
