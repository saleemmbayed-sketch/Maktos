## Company Positioning

- From outsourcing to intelligent revenue orchestration.
- Built for industrial equipment and complex industries in global markets.
- Revenue-as-a-Service capability enabled by best-in-class technology.
- Global reach with native-speaking local expertise.
- Client-specialized teams embedded into customer-facing sales organizations.

## Best-Fit ICP

Target accounts should usually be:

- Industrial equipment manufacturers.
- Complex B2B manufacturers with international sales coverage needs.
- Companies with distributed sales teams, dealer networks, or regional market gaps.
- Organizations using or modernizing CRM for pipeline visibility and forecasting.
- Businesses with high-value equipment, aftermarket, service, parts, or project sales motions.

Likely buying committee:

- Chief Sales Officer.
- VP Sales / Head of Sales.
- Head of Aftermarket / Service Sales.
- CRM / Salesforce owner.
- Revenue Operations leader.
- Regional sales director.
- Managing director for industrial business units.

## Messaging Principles

Use language that is:

- Specific to industrial sales, complex buying cycles, installed base, aftermarket, and global coverage.
- Evidence-led, but careful with claims.
- Operational and executive-friendly.
- Focused on pipeline, coverage, CRM adoption, forecast visibility, and decision-maker access.

Avoid:

- Generic SaaS growth language.
- Consumer-style viral growth tactics.
- Unverified ROI promises.
- Autonomous campaign execution language.
- Claims that imply guaranteed revenue outcomes.

## Strategic Themes

- Global market coverage without building every local team from scratch.
- Native-speaking inside sales embedded into existing sales organizations.
- Better CRM adoption, pipeline hygiene, and forecast visibility.
- Decision-maker data that sales teams can use immediately.
- Industrial revenue orchestration across identify, win, and grow motions.
